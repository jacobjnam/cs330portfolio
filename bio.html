Biography

Background Info

Hello! Welcome to my personal portfolio website. My name is Jacob Nam and I am currently a fourth-year studying for my B.S. in Computer Science at Northwestern University. I am a Korean American, with both of my parents immigrating from Korea. I have moved around the East Coast quite often as a child, being born in Pennsylvania, briefly living in Georgia, and currently residing in New Jersey. I am based in Evanston while at school and in New Jersey while staying with family (during winter and summer breaks).


Background in Computer Science

I was exposed to computer science from middle school, working with Scratch to create simple games. The syntax was in the form of blocks that made writing code easy enough for a 13-year-old (e.g., an “if” statement would be its own block and had slots for the condition and function blocks). Writing programs in Scratch was like a puzzle to me: a puzzle that was more difficult, but as a result, more rewarding. I had fun with the coding assignments that we were given, consistently going beyond the teacher’s expectations. Unfortunately, however, my coding journey took a hiatus as my high school didn’t offer any computer science courses. Subsequently, the courses I took in high school focused on more “traditional” sciences, such as physics and chemistry.

By the time I began applying to colleges, my interest in computer science had died, but I still had no idea what I wanted to study. I applied to several schools that had a strong reputation in engineering, selecting “Undecided/Undeclared” for my preferred major. Come sophomore year, I still hadn’t decided my major. I took an introductory computer science course at the recommendation of my academic advisor and rekindled my interest in coding. After fall term, I officially declared my major.

I recognize that my background in computer science is nowhere near as extensive as some of my peers; I began studying in the field fairly late, relative to my classmates. I fall short in terms of experience and projects, but I am constantly making efforts to close the gap.
